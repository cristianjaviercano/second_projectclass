R&D+i Typologies & IP Strategy

Distinguishing between investment and innovation, protecting intangible assets, and mastering the art of technical drafting.

1. Investment vs. R&D+i

In Process Engineering, understanding the nature of risk is key. Traditional projects scale known technology; R&D+i projects create new knowledge.

Investment Project

Aims to create or expand installed capacity to produce goods using known technologies.

  • Risk: Commercial / Operational
  • Focus: Efficiency & Capacity
  • Ex: Building a new chemical plant

R&D+i Project

Aims to generate new knowledge, develop prototypes, or significantly improve processes.

  • Risk: Technological / Epistemic
  • Focus: Novelty & Knowledge
  • Ex: Developing a new polymer catalyst

Systematic Creative Work

Undertaken to increase the stock of knowledge.

Basic Research Theoretical/Experimental work without specific application.
Applied Research Original investigation directed towards a specific practical objective.

From Knowledge to Design

Application of research findings to a plan or design for new/improved materials, products, or processes before commercial production.

Prototypes Pilot Plants Beta Versions

Market Introduction

Implementation of a new or significantly improved product or process.

Process Innovation New delivery or production methods (e.g., Batch to Continuous).
Product Innovation Goods/Services with significantly improved characteristics.

2. Intellectual Property Strategy

Turning intangible knowledge into a monopoly right.

Criteria for Patentability

1. Novelty

New worldwide. Not part of the "State of the Art".

2. Inventive Step

Not obvious to a person skilled in the art.

3. Industrial App

Can be made or used in an industry.

Utility Model

The Disclosure Dilemma

A critical error is publishing a scientific article before filing the patent application.

The Rule

Public disclosure destroys Novelty.
